A Scientist's Triumph: Nambi Narayanan's Odyssey to the Stars

Nambi Narayanan is a former Indian scientist and aerospace engineer who played a crucial role in the development of India's space program. Born on December 12, 1941, in Nagercoil, Tamil Nadu, he worked at the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and was associated with the development of the Vikas Engine, which powers the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV). After studying mechanical engineering in Madurai, Narayanan started his career in 1966 at ISRO as a technical assistant at the Thumba Equatorial Rocket Launching Station. He was sent to Princeton University on deputation at Government of India's expense in 1969. He completed his master's program there in chemical rocket propulsion under professor Luigi Crocco. He returned to India with expertise in liquid propulsion at a time when Indian rocketry was still solely dependent on solid propellants. He has claimed that he had to educate Sarabhai on liquid propulsion technology in his book. However, Nambi Narayanan's life took a dramatic turn when he was falsely implicated in a spy scandal in 1994. He was accused of selling classified information to foreign agents, leading to his arrest. The charges against him were later found to be baseless, and the case against him was closed by the Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI). Nambi Narayanan's story highlights the challenges and setbacks faced by individuals in the pursuit of scientific excellence and the need for justice when false accusations threaten one's reputation

  • Vikas Engine Contribution: Nambi Narayanan played a crucial role in the development of the Vikas Engine, a liquid rocket engine that powers the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV). PSLV has been a workhorse for the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) in launching satellites into various orbits.
  • Leadership in ISRO: Nambi Narayanan held important positions in ISRO, demonstrating leadership in the organization's efforts to advance space research and technology.
  • March 2019: Padma Bhushan, India's third-highest civilian award
  • Satellite Launch Success: The Vikas Engine, under Narayanan's contributions, has been instrumental in the success of multiple satellite launch missions conducted by ISRO, contributing significantly to India's space capabilities.
